Chart Explanation

Understanding the Chart

The chart shows you the possibilities of succeeding or failing each year. It gives a clear image of whether your balance have more than $0 at the end of the year. The horizontal axis tells you the years and the vertical axis tells you the percentage of succeeding. This chart is formulated by using the Monte Carlo simulation.

What is Monte Carlo Simulation?

Monte Carlo simulation helps you plan for retirement. It allows you to visualize how you would benefit each year by knowing the maximum amount of money to withdraw from balance so that you would not have any conflict dealing with the mark uncertainties. For the stock market, we use the annual returns of the S&P 500 Index (1928-2020). We obtain our AAA bond annual return and T bill from New York University's database (from 1928-2020).  Monte Carlo simulation determines the success rate by randomly select a combination of ratings from our database and then calculate when will the balance exhaust. The simulation runs for 10,000 times and uses all the data gathered to determine the chance for you to have money to spend at the end of the year.
